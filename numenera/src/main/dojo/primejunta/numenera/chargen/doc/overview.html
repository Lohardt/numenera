Overview

The Character Creation Utility is for creating characters for Monte Cook Games' Numenera pen-and-paper role-playing game. You can create characters, advance them, and produce a printable character record for them. A lot of this probably won't make sense if you're not familiar with it. I'm assuming you have the Corebook and refer to it whenever you're confused; I'm not defining Numenera terms here.

It requires a modern HTML5-capable browser such as Google Chrome, Apple Safari, or Mozilla Firefox. It will also work off-line on Chrome and Safari.

Buttons

The main application controls are in the button row at top.

Clear

Closes the current character without saving, and returns to the splash pane.

Print

Opens the Print View. This is a printable character sheet. It should come out OK by printing it directly from your browser. Play with the scaling and fit to page options if you're having trouble. Click anywhere to return to the main view.

Tip: If you're using Firefox, it's a good idea to switch off the page headers and footers.

Play

Opens the Play View. It lets you track stat pools in play, take notes, quickly switch between party members, and do other in-play related stuff. It has its own help text. Please check it out too.

Save

Saves your character to your browser's local data store.

Open

Opens the Character Manager, where you can:

  • Display a list of links to your characters for sharing with other people
  • Delete characters from the browser's data store
  • Download a backup of your characters
  • Restore characters from a backup
  • Connect your Google Drive™, which you can also use for backups and to sync your characters between devices

Advance

Advances the character. Click this when you've finished with character creation and are ready to go into play, and every time you're ready to advance a tier. It won't do anything if the prerequisites aren't met.

After you've finalized your character, you can advance her from the Advancement pane. Enter XP into the XP field, make your choices in the currently active tier, Apply them, and make any stat adjustments you need to do. You can also swap out lower-tier abilities or customize your focus every time you go up a tier. After you've finished applying your changes and have enough XP to advance, click Advance again, and repeat until out of tiers.

GM Controls

Unlocks GM controls, which let you freely adjust stats, add special abilities, and change your character's Descriptor or Focus, even after she has advanced tiers.

Tip: The Abilities pane has a GM control under the Additional Characteristics field. This will let you add Ⓐctions, Ⓔnablers, Ⓣtrained or Ⓢpecialized skills, Ⓜutations, and ︎Ⓘnabilities. If you use the the same name as a feature which affects stats, e.g. Ⓔ Ward or Ⓘ Weakness in Might, they will get applied in Play View or Print View.

Customize (only for un-Advanced characters)

Opens the Character Customizer, which lets you apply the optional Character Customization rules. This control will not be displayed for characters that have been advanced to play-ready. You can still customize the character's Focus from the Advancement tab every time you go up a tier.

How do I add another weapon?

Just enter it under Additional Equipment on its own line. If you include the same phrase as for your starting weapons (e.g. Heavy Bladed Weapon: Greatsword), it'll show up under Attacks on the Print and Play views.

What do those circled letters mean?

Ⓣrained, Ⓢpecialized, Ⓔnabler, Ⓐbility, Ⓘnability, Ⓛight, Ⓜedium, Ⓗeavy, Ⓑashing, Ⓒutting (or Ⓒleaving if you prefer), Ⓡanged, and ⓐsset.

I substituted Ⓒutting for ⓑladed because Ⓑashing also starts with a Ⓑ.
