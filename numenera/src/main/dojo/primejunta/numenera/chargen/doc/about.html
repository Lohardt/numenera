About the Character Creation Utility

The Character Creation Utility is for creating characters for Monte Cook Games' Numenera pen-and-paper role-playing game. You can create characters, advance them, and produce a printable character record for them.

Isn't there an official app for this? If so, why another one?

There certainly is. I made this mostly because it was fun, and coding up the game mechanics was a good way to learn them. However, I also wanted something that produced printable character sheets, since I don't like people messing with their personal electronics while playing.

How do I use it?

Make a hero. Store her with the "Save" button. Once you're ready to advance her, click on the "Advance" button at bottom right. This locks in her characteristics and you can only modify them according to the rules. You can change possessions and notes though.

Note: You can bookmark each of your heroes individually, for example on an iPad desktop, or manage them with the Saved Characters feature.

"Ward" is supposed to improve my Armor but I don't see it in the stats pane. What gives?

Check the printable character sheet. Bonuses from selected abilities like esoteries and mutations will get applied to the character sheet, but they won't yet appear in the "editor." If I've missed something, please let me know.

What do those circled letters mean?

Ⓣrained, Ⓢpecialized, Ⓔnabler, Ⓐbility, Ⓘnability, Ⓛight, Ⓜedium, Ⓗeavy, Ⓑashing, Ⓒutting (or Ⓒleaving if you prefer), Ⓡanged, and ⓐsset.

I substituted Ⓒutting for ⓑladed because Ⓑashing also starts with a Ⓑ. (Personally I would've picked Ⓒrushing, Ⓑladed, and Ⓡanged, but eh.)

What's with the splats on some descriptors, like Mutant*?

They're from the optional rules section of the Corebook, and it's up to your GM to allow them (or not), and they need more work to set up. You can set your mutant's mutations, but you need to look them up. There are sometimes more fields available than you need; just leave the ones you don't need empty, and they'll go away once you advance your hero. If you make up your own mutations with stat penalties, just enter negative values into the boxes. They're provided for Beneficial and Distinctive mutations.

What is it made of?

It is an offline-capable all-Javascript/HTML/CSS webapp written with the Dojo Toolkit.

Tip: If you're on an iPad and make a link on your desktop for it (the button just to the left of the address field), it will run in full-screen, which is kind of nice. You can even bookmark each of your characters this way.

It doesn't work on my browser!

I test this (not as well as I'd like) on Google Chrome, Mozilla Firefox, and Apple Safari, on a Mac and an iPad, and occasionally on an oldish Samsung tablet running Android. That's about all I can manage. If you're running some other browser and something doesn't work, please submit a patch.

N.b.: If/when the licensing issues are sorted out, I want to put this on GitHub, at which point this will become much easier.

Do you collect any data?

No, other than usual web server traffic logging. If you link to characters you've shared, that request and its query string does get logged in the web server's HTTP logs.

So where do my saved characters go?

In your browser's local data store. That means they'll only be available on the browser you used to create them. You can share them by copy-pasting links, though.

Will I lose the characters if I empty my history, clear my cache, and remove my cookies?

The details depend on your browser, but yeah, you most likely will.

Any way I can "back up" my characters?

Bookmark the "Share (character name)" link. Also don't trust random things on the Internet, so make prints and/or save them as PDF's. I don't want responsibility for keeping your characters safe!

Any gotchas with the printable character sheet?

It seems to work OK on Firefox, Safari, and Google Chrome at least. Just turn off the headers and footers in Firefox.

I found a way to cheat!

Well bully for you. The validation checks on your character are intended to stop innocent mistakes. You can probably find ways around them if you try. In any case it's up to your GM to approve any character you roll up.

That said, if you find a mistake or omission in the checks, please let me know and I'll see if I can fix it.

Is this an official Numenera product?

No way! Numenera is © Monte Cook Games LLC. The Character Creation Utility is an unofficial fan project by me, Petteri Sulonen. I may take it off-line at any time, for any reason, in particular if MCG asks me to, so don't rely on it too much.

Why isn't this on GitHub?

Because Monte Cook Games hasn't yet come up with a license for fan created content and I don't want to tread on their toes. I believe that what I'm doing here falls under 'fair use' as I'm only using mechanics (which can't be copyrighted) and individual phrases (which are too small to be copyrighted), and in any case the thing's fairly useless for anyone who isn't already playing Numenera, or wants to. However, I like them and if they feel I'm doing them harm, I will take this down.
