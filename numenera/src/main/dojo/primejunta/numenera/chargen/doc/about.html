About the Character Creation Utility

The Character Creation Utility is for creating characters for Monte Cook Games' Numenera pen-and-paper role-playing game. You can create characters, advance them, and produce a printable character record for them.

How do I use it?

Make a hero. Store her with the button. Once you're ready to take her into play, click on the Advance button at top right. This locks in her characteristics and you can only modify them according to the rules. You can change possessions and notes though.

Can I back up or save my characters?

Yes, and you should. The backup features are in the Character Manager pane behind the button. You can download backups into files, or if you have a Google Drive™ account, you can back them up to that. In fact Google Drive is currently the only practical backup method if you're using an iPad, which doesn't really have file download and upload features.

You want access to my Google Drive?!?

It's the only way I'm able to send your backups there. But I understand if you don't trust me. See the Privacy Policy for more on this.

Sharing characters

If you have a Google Drive, you can import and export characters to and from any devices you use using the Character Manager's backup to cloud and restore from cloud features.

If you want to share a character publicly or with someone else, open the Character Manager pane from the button. You can display each of the characters as links from there. These are normal Web links, which you can bookmark, mail, post on a forum message, or whatever.

What's the little button?

It's an in-play view of the character. You can do some stuff like track stat pool depletion, do recovery rolls and such. It has its own help pane in the tab.

"Ward" is supposed to improve my Armor but I don't see it in the stats pane. What gives?

Check the Play View or the Print View. Bonuses from selected abilities like esoteries and mutations will get applied to the character sheet, but they won't yet appear in the "editor." If I've missed something, please let me know.

What do those circled letters mean?

Ⓣrained, Ⓢpecialized, Ⓔnabler, Ⓐbility, Ⓘnability, Ⓛight, Ⓜedium, Ⓗeavy, Ⓑashing, Ⓒutting (or Ⓒleaving if you prefer), Ⓡanged, and ⓐsset.

I substituted Ⓒutting for ⓑladed because Ⓑashing also starts with a Ⓑ.

What's with the splats on some descriptors, like Mutant*?

They're from the optional rules section of the Corebook, and it's up to your GM to allow them (or not), and to approve any mutations your mutant may have. If entering the data manually, there are sometimes more fields available than you need; just leave the ones you don't need empty, and they'll go away once you advance your hero. If you make up your own mutations with stat penalties, just enter negative values into the boxes.

Isn't there an official app for this? If so, why another one?

There certainly is. I made this mostly because it was fun, and coding up the game mechanics was a good way to learn them. However, I also wanted something that produced printable character sheets, since I don't like people messing with their personal electronics while playing.

What is it made of?

It is an offline-capable all-Javascript/HTML/CSS webapp written with the Dojo Toolkit.

Tip: If you're on an iPad and make a link on your desktop for it (the button just to the left of the address field), it will run in full-screen, which is kind of nice. You can even bookmark each of your characters this way.

I found a bug/omission/error some other issue!

Let me know about it (see below), and I'll see what I can do.

It doesn't work on my browser!

I test this (not as well as I'd like) on Google Chrome, Mozilla Firefox, and Apple Safari, on a Mac and an iPad, and occasionally on an oldish Samsung tablet running Android. That's about all I can manage. If you want to submit a patch, do it on GitHub.

Do you collect any data?

No, other than usual web server traffic logging. If you link to characters you've shared, that request and its query string does get logged in the web server's HTTP logs. Backups (to file or to Google Drive) do not go through my web server at all, so I'll never know about those.

So where do my saved characters go?

In your browser's local data store. That means they'll only be available on the browser you used to create them. You can share them by copy-pasting links, syncing with Google Drive, or downloading and uploading backup files (from the pane).

Will I lose the characters if I empty my history, clear my cache, and remove my cookies?

The details depend on your browser, but yeah, you most likely will.

Any gotchas with the printable character sheet?

It seems to work OK on Firefox, Safari, and Google Chrome at least. Just turn off the headers and footers in Firefox.

How closely does this follow the Rules as Written?

I've done my best to code in the rules as written. In case of ambiguity, I've erred on the side of greater permissiveness, on the grounds that the GM can always restrict your choices whereas she can't do much about the app. I've also left as much open as possible, so for example you can adjust shins and add or remove equipment freely, make up your own skills and mutations and so on.

One fairly important thing is stated two ways in the Corebook. On page 24 it says that you can purchase one of several alternatives instead of training in a skill when advancing your character. On page 112 it says that purchasing one of the alternatives counts toward any of your tier advancement requirements. I've used the stricter interpretation, because I didn't notice the other one until it was too late. Now it would be difficult to change without breaking character compatibility, so I probably won't.

The single most debatable call I've made has to do with specialization in a combat skill. The rules governing this are somewhat complex and somewhat ambiguous, with some fairly strict restrictions on who is allowed to specialize in an attack or defense skill, and when.

The basic version is simple enough: glaives get attack skills and jacks get defense skills; they get to train in them from tier 2 and 1 on respectively, and specialize from tier 5 on. However, some descriptors and foci also grant attack and defense skills "for free." So what do we do, say, with a Graceful jack who gets Speed Defense from Graceful?

A strict reading of the rules states that the jack is not allowed to take Speed Defense on tiering up until Tier 5, because she is already trained in it. However, this leads to a number of problems. Therefore, I have interpreted the rules loosely: I allow specialization in a skill if and once you receive it as a bonus from your descriptor or focus. There are three reasons for this call.

First, it maintains character differentiation: our Graceful jack may retain her distinguishing advantage in Speed Defense relative to other characters even as she tiers up, and the other characters are able to pick Speed Defense as a tier perk.

Second, this is not as big a difference with the strict reading as it appears at first glance. Many descriptors and foci grant defense (and sometimes attack) skills that are conditional and definitely do give specialization when the conditions apply; Trained Without Armor for example stacks with Speed Defense, giving Specialized in that when, well, without armor. So allowing "liberal" defense skill stacking doesn't change things as much as it might look at first glance.

And third, enforcing the strict limits would have resulted in some collisions. For example, Masters Defense gets Intellect Defense for free on Tier 4, which jacks can pick as an ability any time before that, but are normally only allowed to specialize in it on Tier 5. So is a jack who picked it earlier specialized when he gets it from his focus, or not? If he is, then would he be allowed to pick it after receiving it from the focus, as a Tier 4 Trick of the Trade or purchased ability? If not, why the difference? In other words, I would have gotten into a mess of more judgment calls and more scripting to handle each potential collision in a fairly big matrix (descriptor, focus, type, tier-up bonus perk, tier-up bought perk). That, I'm fairly certain, would have just made things worse.

In any case, this interpretation, like everything else, must ultimately be arbitrated by your GM and your group. If you want to enforce the strict interpretation, by all means do so – simply don't take any of the problematic skills until the strict reading says you're allowed to.

I found a way to cheat!

Well bully for you. The validation checks on your character are intended to stop innocent mistakes. You can probably find ways around them if you try. In any case it's up to your GM to approve any character you roll up.

That said, if you find a mistake or omission in the checks, please let me know and I'll see if I can fix it.
